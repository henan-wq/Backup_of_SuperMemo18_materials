Title: Greater Yellow-Finch

Fairly common in the Andes of central Chile and adjacent Argentina;
seen readily at ski resorts, around villages, on bogs, and on stony and grassy slopes.

Often in flocks, associates readily with other seed-eating birds.

Feeds mainly on the ground, but sings from prominent perches such as a rock or building.

Not appreciably larger than other yellow-finches, but no similar species in most of range.

May overlap in winter locally with Greenish Yellow-Finch, which has shorter wings and a shorter bill, and male never is as bright golden as the Greater Yellow-Finch.
